# User Personas & Usage Scenarios

## User Persona 1: The Overwhelmed Streamer

**Name:** Sarah, 32
**Occupation:** Marketing Manager
**Location:** San Francisco, CA
**Streaming Subscriptions:** Netflix, Hulu, Disney+, HBO Max, Amazon Prime

### Background

Sarah subscribes to 5 streaming services but spends 20-30 minutes every evening trying to decide what to watch. She often gives up and rewatches comfort shows like The Office. She feels like she's missing great content buried in her services.

### Pain Points

- "I spend more time scrolling than watching"
- "I know there's something perfect for my mood, but I can't find it"
- "Each app only shows me their content, I forget what's on each service"
- "Recommendations are always the same popular stuff"

### Goals

- Find something to watch in under 5 minutes
- Discover hidden gems she wouldn't find through normal browsing
- Search based on her current mood, not just genre
- See what's available across all her services at once

### How Meta-Media-Search Helps

1. Natural language query: "Something uplifting but not cheesy, like Ted Lasso"
2. Visual map shows related shows across all her services
3. Discovers "Schitt's Creek" on Netflix through actor/theme connections
4. Saves 25 minutes of scrolling, finds perfect match

### Success Metrics

- Time to decision: < 5 minutes (was 25 minutes)
- Satisfaction with choice: 9/10
- Discovers content on services she "forgot" she had
- Returns 3-4x per week

- Explore cinematic connections (French New Wave → modern indie)
- Find films by cinematographer, composer, or production designer
- Discover international cinema similar to favorites
- Understand historical context and influences

### How Meta-Media-Search Helps

1. Explores visual map starting from "The 400 Blows" (Truffaut)
2. Graph shows connections to other New Wave directors
3. Discovers modern directors influenced by Truffaut
4. Finds "Boyhood" through theme and style similarity
5. Explores further to discover Iranian cinema connections

### Success Metrics

- Discovers 5+ new films per week
- Explores multi-hop relationships (3+ degrees of separation)
- Adds 20+ films to watchlist monthly
- Shares discovery paths with cinephile friends

## Edge Case Scenarios

### Scenario A: The Vague Memory

**User:** "That movie with the guy from the thing, on a boat or something?"
**Traditional:** Impossible to search
**Meta-Media:** LLM + graph traversal can handle vague queries, asks clarifying questions

### Scenario B: The Complex Combo

**User:** "Sci-fi but character-driven, like Black Mirror but more optimistic"
**Traditional:** No single genre matches
**Meta-Media:** Multi-dimensional similarity finds "Arrival", "Everything Everywhere All at Once"

### Scenario C: The Anti-Recommendation

**User:** "Like Inception but WITHOUT time travel, paradoxes, or Leonardo DiCaprio"
**Traditional:** Can't search by negation
**Meta-Media:** Semantic understanding filters out unwanted elements, finds "Shutter Island" (same director, different themes)

### Scenario D: The Cross-Medium Discovery

**User:** "Games like Bioshock but in movie form"
**Traditional:** Different media, no connection
**Meta-Media:** Theme and mood mapping finds "Dark City", "The City of Lost Children"

## Behavioral Insights

### Discovery Patterns

**Explorers (40%):** Want to wander through relationship graph

- Time spent: 15-30 minutes
- Value: Serendipitous discovery
- Behavior: Multi-hop traversal, saves paths

**Searchers (35%):** Know what they want, need fast lookup

- Time spent: 2-5 minutes
- Value: Speed and accuracy
- Behavior: Direct query, quick decision

**Browsers (25%):** Open-minded, need inspiration

- Time spent: 8-15 minutes
- Value: Curated clusters, not overwhelming
- Behavior: Zoom through mood groups, hover previews

### Engagement Hooks

**"I didn't know I'd love that"** - Surprise discovery metric

- Hidden gems found through 3+ degree connections
- Content outside usual genre preferences
- Cross-cultural discoveries

**"That's exactly what I meant"** - Query understanding satisfaction

- Natural language correctly interpreted
- Nuanced mood/theme matching
- Negative filtering working properly

**"I see the connection now"** - Learning through exploration

- Understanding why two films are related
- Discovering new directors/actors/themes
- Building mental model of film landscape

## Accessibility Scenarios

### User with ADHD

- Needs: Quick, visual overview; not overwhelming; clear next actions
- Meta-Media-Search: Progressive disclosure, visual clustering, quick previews

### User with Visual Impairment

- Needs: Screen reader compatibility, keyboard navigation
- Meta-Media-Search: Semantic HTML, ARIA labels, text-based alternative view

### User with Limited Tech Literacy

- Needs: Intuitive interface, forgiving search
- Meta-Media-Search: Natural language queries, helpful suggestions, undo actions

### Non-Native English Speaker

- Needs: Multilingual support, visual aids
- Meta-Media-Search: Language detection, visual context, translation support
